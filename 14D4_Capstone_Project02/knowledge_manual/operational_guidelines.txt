AfyaPlus Operational Guidelines

1. Travel Reimbursement
   All staff travel claims must be submitted within 30 days of trip completion.
   Reimbursement is processed within 15 business days after approval.

2. Procurement & Equipment Purchases
   Clinic equipment purchases above $500 require director sign-off.
   Any single purchase over $5,000 must go through the Central Procurement Committee.

3. Clinic Shift Management
   Night-shift staff are paid a 20% premium over the standard hourly rate.
   Shift swaps must be approved by the ward supervisor at least 24 hours in advance.

4. Medication Stock Policy
   Critical medications (e.g., amoxicillin, paracetamol) must never drop below 50 units.
   Stock counts are audited every Monday morning.

5. Patient Referral Protocol
   Patients with severe chest pain, difficulty breathing, or heavy bleeding must be
   referred immediately to the nearest Level 5 hospital.
   Routine referrals require a signed form from the attending clinician.

6. Data Privacy & Security
   All patient data must be stored on servers located within Kenya.
   Access to sensitive records requires two-factor authentication.

7. Nurse Rotation Board
   The nursing roster is managed by the Head Nurse and updated every Friday.
   The board is displayed in the staff room and digitally on the intranet.
   Each nurse is assigned to one of three shifts: morning (6am-2pm), afternoon (2pm-10pm), or night (10pm-6am).
   Off-duty days are clearly marked; a nurse may not be scheduled for more than 6 consecutive days.
   Any changes to the roster must be approved by the Head Nurse at least 48 hours in advance.

8. Doctor On-Call Board
   The on-call schedule for doctors is maintained by the Medical Superintendent’s office.
   It lists the primary on-call doctor and a backup for each 24-hour period.
   Doctors on call must be reachable within 15 minutes and are prohibited from leaving the hospital premises.
   The board also indicates which doctors are on leave or post-call rest days.

9. Off Days and Extended Days
   All clinical staff are entitled to at least one full off-day per week.
   Doctors who work a public holiday or cover an extra shift may accrue an “extended day” which can be taken later with supervisor approval.
   Extended days must be used within the same quarter; unused days are forfeited unless a written exemption is granted.

10. Emergency Call Policy for Doctors Staying on Floor
    During an emergency call (e.g., mass casualty, code blue), all off-duty doctors within the facility must report to their assigned floor immediately.
    Doctors already on the floor must remain until the emergency is declared over by the Chief Medical Officer.
    Failure to respond may result in disciplinary review.
    The on-call doctor is responsible for coordinating the emergency response and updating the Medical Superintendent every 30 minutes.
